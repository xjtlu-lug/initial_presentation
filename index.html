The Linux Operating Systems

A brief introduction to our topic

History

  • Originally created by Linus Torvalds in 1991
  • Free (libre) and open-source alternative to Unix
  • Later collaborated with the GNU project for a complete free operating system

Adoption

  • Servers: Most of the websites run on Linux servers
  • Desktop: Hundreds of distributions for daily use
    ...but Torvalds did not have a good time with Jobs :(
  • Mobile: ANDROID!!!!!
  • The online world today CANNOT EXIST without Linux

Advantages

  • Free & Secure: Free as in freedom, no Microsoft and no Big Brother
  • Performant: Only use what you need. Suitable for businesses with high performance requirements

...and for us

  • Learning: Source code available everywhere, read & hack as you like
  • Career: Unavoidable to work with in the IT industry

Free Software

The heart of every LUG

Introduction

  • Belief: Software, like knowledge, should be free
  • Free as in freedom, not in free beer
  • Nobody should be able to control my computing device, nor should they impose any sort of surveillience on me

Importance

  • Linux: The biggest free and open source software (FOSS) project
  • BSD: Even Apple macOS and iOS are using a free software implementation of BSD at its core

...for users

  • Academic: LaTeX & more professional tools
  • Daily: Chrome is partially free while Firefox is completely free
  • The Internet and its openness is built on free software and free technologies

Linux User Group

The worldwide user community of Linux

Who we are (again)

  • Linux & Unix users / learners / enthusiasts
  • Free software advocators
  • Followers of latest technologies

What is LUG

  • Organization of Linux users
  • Often found in universities, large cities and localities.
  • Similar to Unix User Group
  • Academic, Innovative and Enthusiastic
  • In China: BLUG (Beijing LUG), Wuhan LUG, Chongqing LUG, LUG@USTC, LUG@NEU

Objectives

  • To promote the use of Linux and other Unix-like operating systems
  • To increase involvement of XJTLU students in free software communities
  • To build a platform for sharing technologies related to Linux and the FOSS world
  • To prepare XJTLU students for future careers with Linux-related environment
  • To motivate XJTLU students to become world citizens through activity in FOSS community

Activities (Proposed)

  • Installation parties: For those who would like to have a taste of Linux
  • Linux guide seminars: Guiding newbies through Linux
  • FOSS developement: We will have FOSS projects / services for XJTLU students and for everyone
  • Routine talks / meetups: internal sharing of knowledge and information related to the club's topic (like TUNight of TUNA)
  • Lectures: Extended talks. For everyone in XJTLU. (based on how LUG develops)
